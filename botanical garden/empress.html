Empress Botanical Gardens

The Empress Botanical Gardens in Pune, Maharashtra, established in 1880, is a historic green oasis named after Queen Victoria, the Empress of India. Spanning 39 acres, the garden is home to a diverse collection of tropical trees, colorful flower beds, and beautifully landscaped lawns, offering visitors a peaceful retreat amidst nature. It also features a well-maintained nursery with a variety of plants available for sale and regularly hosts seasonal flower shows and exhibitions. Beyond its scenic beauty, the garden plays a key role in botanical research and plant conservation, serving as an educational center for horticulture while also supporting local biodiversity. A cherished landmark in Pune, the Empress Botanical Gardens continues to be a popular destination for nature lovers and tourists alike
