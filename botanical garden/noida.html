Botanical Garden of Indian Republic

The Botanical Garden of Indian Republic in Noida, Uttar Pradesh, is a vast 163.5-acre sanctuary dedicated to the conservation of rare and endangered plant species. It houses over 10,000 species, including medicinal herbs, exotic flowers, and native Indian plants, making it a biodiversity hotspot. Divided into specialized sections like the medicinal plant garden, butterfly conservatory, and aquatic plant area, the garden promotes environmental sustainability through eco-friendly practices such as water harvesting. It also serves as an educational hub, offering workshops, guided tours, and programs to raise awareness about biodiversity and conservation. Ideal for nature lovers, students, and researchers, the Botanical Garden is both a peaceful retreat and a crucial center for environmental education in India.
