National Botanic Garden of India

The National Botanic Garden of India, located in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, is a prestigious botanical research center and conservation hub managed by the National Botanical Research Institute (NBRI). Spanning 70 acres, the garden is dedicated to the preservation of Indiaâ€™s rich floral biodiversity and houses a vast collection of over 5,000 species of plants, including medicinal plants, rare orchids, and endangered species. It features specialized sections like a cactus house, fernery, and a rose garden, attracting both researchers and tourists alike. As a center for botanical research, it plays a crucial role in the study of plant taxonomy, ecology, and conservation. The National Botanic Garden of India also serves as an educational space, offering guided tours and environmental programs to raise awareness about the importance of plant conservation and sustainable development.
