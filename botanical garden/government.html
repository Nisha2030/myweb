Government Botanical Gardens

The Government Botanical Gardens in Ooty, Tamil Nadu, is a renowned horticultural treasure spread across 55 acres at the foothills of the Nilgiri Mountains. Established in 1848, this garden is home to over 650 species of plants, including rare ferns, vibrant flowers, and century-old trees, making it a key attraction for nature enthusiasts and tourists. Divided into distinct sections such as the fern house, Italian garden, and conservatory, the garden also features a fossilized tree trunk estimated to be around 20 million years old. Known for its annual flower show, the Government Botanical Gardens serves as a center for botanical research and plant conservation, promoting awareness about the rich floral diversity of the region while offering visitors a serene escape into nature.
